Short Story

The ticking clock filled the classroom as the teacher headed up to the board. He grabbed a piece of bright yellow chalk that shrieked as it rubbed against the aluminum tray and began to write. Behind the teacher's back, columns of desks filled with seniors took off their statue costumes and moved around to whisper to one another. One very quiet young man sat leaned back in the farthest desk from the board. His head still as a rock while his eyes explored the room in boredom. Beside him, classmates discussed attending college and what they wanted to study which made the man’s eyes roll and his head begin to turn away. A classmate beside him caught the movement, and turned away from a conversation. “What about you Zas? What do you want to study?” Zaslow, with his head turned to the farthest reaches of classroom, shrugged. There was a blank look on Zaslow’s face as he asked himself that same question. He Thought for a second, but the blank look grew stronger.

The rattling chatter of metal on metal broke the silence in the room and all the students rushed to leave. Zaslow didn’t move, and just sat at his desk while his eyes moved to the view out of the room’s windows. In the distance past the edge of the school's campus, trees tried to hide a small waterfall that dropped into a river below the road. Zaslow watched the waterfall as the little thought of climbing the rocks came into his mind. When the incoming students broke Zaslow’s gaze, he sprung up and paced out to the hall with a half smile on his face.

The harsh metal bell had rung once more. The busy streets through the school became backed up by the rush to lunch. Zaslow didn’t like the pushing and shoving, always avoiding it by walking to the basement. Turning a corner at the end of the hallway, Zas stuck his foot out to boot a bright red door open and let gravity carry his body down the stairs. As his feet land on the basement’s tiles, Zas walks back under the stairs and drops his weighty bag. Zas falls to the ground and rips open the zipper of his. His hands extract a beaten up notebook, and a stubby number two pencil. Zas pushes back up against the brick under the stairs and starts writing.

Deep in a forest, the crashing of a waterfall is silenced by the guardians that tower above all life. These ancient trees stretch their roots far and wide to form the life of the forest, the blood vessels. At the forest's heart, the only sound is that of the rushing water. Water crashes I pan my head towards the edge of the waterfall. Beside the bright water rushing over the edge, lies a rock formation that rises to the top and extends back out to the forest. I jump up and start climbing.

The crack of of the rock breaks the sound of the waterfall beside me. I take a breath, and I feel my heart accelerate. My left hand tightens as it races to clinch the jagged slate by the edge. Water droplets fall upon the rocks, and flow down to my hands. My right hand starts to slip, so I swing it around to my back where it dips in a pouch of white chalk. A grunt leaves my mouth as my elbow bends to raise my dangling body back up. When my right hand returns its grip to the wall, a snap echoes down and through the trees. My left arm loosens, and the rock crumbles between my fingers. I struggle to catch something else, but my arm is weak and falls to my side. My body sways against the cliff, oscillating under my chalk stained hand. The muscles in my arms stretch, while my head turns down in disappointment to peer at the height below. I slow my breathing, and my heart rate paces itself. With that regained energy, I unlock my elbow and clench my tendons. As my eyes rise to to sky, the water drops stick to my glasses and run down my cheeks with a refreshing feel.

From the gods above, a piece of a soaked leaf slapped his forehead as his foot stomped the ground beside the roots. His eyes blinked, regenerating focus for the climb ahead.
