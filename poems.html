Poems

Frisbee

Cleats ruffle the blades of grass.
They stop hard and dig beneath the green.
Toes emerge, and toss away a piece of Earth.
Wrist flicks with the crack of a bone.
It’s heard all the way from the field to home.
The silver disk cuts through the air,
And whistles under the wind's breath.
Overhead, the sun shines bright.
Its rays beam down, and illuminate the silver surfer.

Tired

I’m always tired,
Even if I don’t look like it.
Tired because of work,
Tired because of sports,
Tired because of school.
It runs across my arms and legs.
It’s fueled by late nights,
And refreshed by early mornings.
It clings to my eyes all week,
And disappears when the week ends.
Midnight clicks in,
Monday arrives.
I’m tired again.

My Name

My name is different,
And I like that.
It’s hard to pronounce,
But that makes it fun.
So many nicknames.
I always laugh.
Past down through generations,
I’m proud to still bear it.
My name made me who I am,
And who I will be.
