Closing Letter

So this is it. The end is here.

Throughout this class my dislike of writing has evolved into a sense of appreciation. What really changed my perspective was the ability to write whatever I wanted despite being based on shallow guidelines. Every Afternoon I have loved sharing my own thoughts and creative prowess in my writer's notebook. Each one of my prompts reflects an aspect of my life. I really wanted every piece of my writing to contain a piece of myself in it to show who I am to the reader. With the mention of show not tell in our writing, I really pushed to incorporate that into every piece I wrote. Over time, my writer’s notebook prompts utilized more sensory detail to introduce objects and avoided telling the reader directly. My writer’s notebook entries “A New President” and “Ascending” used many details to introduce the two main objects in each scene. These entire pieces are spent building up a descriptive and vivid image of two unique things for their full reveal in the end. The long pieces of writing were some of my most difficult and became the most aggravating ones to write. I found it hard to tell a long story despite my lengthy planning to overcome that struggle. I struggled in creating many of the pieces in this portfolio. Often times it becomes difficult for my mind to convey ideas and get them through the pencil and onto the paper. Writing does not come fast to me, but I’m not bothered by it. Despite all the struggling I had and what poor pieces I believe I produced, I am proud. My writing is representation of who I am, and that is all I can ask for.

Goodbye MPF
